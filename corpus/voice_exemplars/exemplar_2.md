# Exemplar 2 — Lyrical and elegiac

*Style note: longer breath, allows the magic system to surface. For episodes that follow a Tide-witch working or an emotional reveal.*

---

Previously, on *The Tides of Vassen*.

The Bay does not give up its dead. They lie in the black silt, weighted in their shrouds, and the tide passes over them twice a day in slow benediction. We do not visit them. We send them down, and we walk away, and we say their names afterward only by accident.

Sergeant Verity Crane has visited them. She has done this all her life. Her grandmother told her, on a moonlit beach when she was fourteen, that this was what she would do, and that her voice would leave her for it. She has not yet been afraid of that.

In the second hour of the second night, a hooded figure stood at her door and wrote on a folded slip of paper that her mother would die in agony before the moon set unless she walked to the Bay and spoke to a particular shade. She walked. She spoke. The shade was the clerk who had been carved upon. He answered her, in a woman's voice, with a stanza of verse.

She wrote it down. She did not know whose voice it was.

Inspector Hale, that same night, stood on the Lighthouse point and held his wife's wedding ring in his fist, and did not, for the first time in five years, say her name aloud.

A lantern in the Cathedral of the Drowned Mother guttered, recovered, and held.

The tide came in an inch farther than the day before.
