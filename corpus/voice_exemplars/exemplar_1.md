# Exemplar 1 — Cold and procedural

*Style note: brisk pacing, beats stated flat, almost a constabulary report. For episodes that open after action-heavy hours.*

---

Previously, on *The Tides of Vassen*.

A clerk was found in the Eastern District. No one is found in the Eastern District. The wall is patrolled. The Watch shoots. He was there anyway. A verse was cut into him. Five lines, careful work, made on a moonlit night.

Inspector Hale knew the verse. His wife wrote it. His wife has been dead five years.

The Dredgemaster lied to him. The Compass moved. The Dredgemaster covered the needle with his hand and said nothing.

That night Hale's wedding ring went missing from the evidence locker. Brass, plain. Twenty-one years on his finger. Gone in the time it took to glove up.

Sergeant Crane was at the scene with him. She read the verse over his shoulder. She did not recognize it.

She received a letter at her door before dawn. Her mother, the letter said, was worsening. Councilor Thane had increased his patronage. The handwriting was her physician's. The handwriting was, she would learn, not.

The tide came in an inch farther than the day before. It always does.

A lantern, somewhere on a wall in the Lower Wards, went out in the wind.
