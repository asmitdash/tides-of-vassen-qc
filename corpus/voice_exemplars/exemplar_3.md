# Exemplar 3 — Fragmented, breathless, late-season

*Style note: short cuts, image-led, near-silent. For the recap before the finale, when language itself has begun to fail.*

---

Previously.

A clerk. A corporal. A boy named Joss, in a flooded basement, the third stanza fresh on his chest.

A book stamped with a sigil. A girl on a rooftop. A coil of climber's line.

The Compass, picked up from the Salt Exchange steps before dawn. The Compass, taken back in an alley.

Inspector Hale, on a beach in the moonlight, watching his partner of seven years speak with the dead.

His wife's voice in the mouth of a clerk who never met her.

Sergeant Crane, on the same beach, finding she could no longer say his name.

A ledger under a girl's floorboards. A hand in a glass jar in a Councilor's locked cabinet. A wedding ring in a velvet pouch in a desk drawer.

Three lanterns out, one still burning.

The tide came in.

Tonight.
