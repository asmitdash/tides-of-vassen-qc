# THE TIDES OF VASSEN
## Episode 4 — "The Tide Witch"

---

# SCENE 1 — EXT. SOUTHERN BEACH — A FISHER'S LANTERN — PRE-DAWN

A weathered iron lantern on a pole at the high-water mark, lit by the early fishermen. The wick is fat and yellow. A gust off the harbor takes it. Out.

The fisher curses. He does not relight it. He shoulders his net and walks on.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 2 — INT. HARBOR PRECINCT — INTERROGATION ROOM — MORNING

The wounded Watchman from the basement, his right wrist bandaged, his left in iron cuffs. He sits at a plain table. He does not look up.

Hale across from him. Verity at the door, taking notes in her oilcloth book.

HALE
Tell me.

WATCHMAN
A warrant.

HALE
You'll have one.

WATCHMAN
Then I'll wait for it.

HALE
You shot at me in the dark.

WATCHMAN
You shot at me.

HALE
I shot at you because I had to. You were going for iron.

WATCHMAN
A constable's warrant doesn't reach the Watch.

HALE
A magistrate's does.

WATCHMAN
I'll wait for that, then.

A beat. Hale slides a folded piece of paper across the table.

HALE
A verse.

WATCHMAN
Don't read.

HALE
A stanza of a longer poem. Six lines. The third in a sequence. Carved on a salt-runner's brother yesterday.

WATCHMAN
I'll wait for the magistrate.

HALE
The Council's man came through the locker after the first body and took my ring.

A beat.

WATCHMAN
I don't know what you're talking about.

HALE
I think you do.

The Watchman does not answer. Hale watches him a long time.

HALE
Sergeant. Step out.

VERITY
Inspector—

HALE
Step out, Sergeant.

She closes her notebook. She goes. She closes the door.

Hale leans across the table. He does not raise his voice.

HALE
A man like you doesn't keep a Councilor's secrets out of love. A man like you keeps them because he has been paid, or because he is afraid. I will pay better than the Council. I will not arrange for you to die in a cistern.

The Watchman, after a long moment, lifts his eyes. He looks at the door.

WATCHMAN
Pay how.

HALE
Out of Vassen. Tonight. New name. Inland. Aelmar's a wide kingdom.

A beat.

WATCHMAN
The Compass.

HALE
What about it.

WATCHMAN
Locked. Iron-roofed vault. Council Chamber.

HALE
Who put it there.

WATCHMAN
The Eastern Ward.

HALE
A Councilor.

WATCHMAN
Don't make me say the name in this room.

HALE
A name.

A pause. The Watchman takes the folded paper Hale slid across earlier. He turns it over. He writes one word on the back, with a stub of pencil from his own pocket.

He pushes it back.

Hale reads. He folds it. He puts it inside his coat lining.

HALE
Out of Vassen tonight.

WATCHMAN
Tonight.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 3 — INT. SISTER'S VESTRY — CATHEDRAL OF THE DROWNED MOTHER — LATER MORNING

The Sister from Episode 2 again. The pewter cup. The cold stone.

Hale puts a folded paper on the table.

HALE
A magistrate's warrant. The seal is dry.

The Sister picks it up. She reads. She sets it down.

SISTER
The name in our confessional record was given by Eline Hale herself, in a private confession of June 1898, three weeks before her death. She came to confess that she had given a copy of her 1897 verses, in her own hand, to one person besides yourself. She named that person in confession.

HALE
Who.

SISTER
A copyist's contract. The Salt Exchange. A senior auditor.

HALE
A name, Sister.

SISTER
Thelonius Thane.

A long beat. Hale, who already knew it from the Watchman's hand in his pocket, does not move.

HALE
Why him.

SISTER
She did not say why. She said, *because he asked.* That was all.

Hale lifts the warrant from the table. He folds it.

HALE
Did she seem afraid.

SISTER
She seemed tired.

A pause.

HALE
She came alone.

SISTER
Yes.

HALE
She did not tell me.

SISTER
No.

He nods, once. He goes.

The Sister turns to the seawater cup and dips her finger and crosses, very small, the line of her own throat.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 4 — INT. HARBOR PRECINCT — HALE'S OFFICE — MIDDAY

Verity at the door. Her notebook in her hand. She speaks, when she speaks, in a voice that has gone roughly an octave lower than her normal register.

VERITY
A man asking for me at the front desk. Says he's from my mother's physician.

HALE
Receive him.

VERITY
Inspector.

HALE
What.

VERITY
Don't follow.

A beat.

HALE
Sergeant.

VERITY
Don't.

She turns. She goes.

He waits. He counts to thirty in his head. Then he stands. He puts on his coat. He follows.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 5 — EXT. PRECINCT YARD — MIDDAY

A side gate. Verity steps through it. She is alone — the man from the front desk handed her a folded slip and left without speaking. She reads it as she walks.

THE SLIP (insert)
*Tonight. Same hour. Same place. The shade of Joss Vance. Last working. Your mother's last night.*

She crumples the slip in her gloved fist. She walks fast.

Behind her, at thirty paces, Hale follows. He has been a constable for twenty-six years. He follows well.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 6 — EXT. LOWER WARDS — A FISH-CELLAR DOOR — AFTERNOON

Mira sits on the step of the fish-cellar that holds her room above. Her shoulder is freshly bandaged — properly, by a Lower Wards woman who has done it twice before. The leather Black Ledger is in a sailcloth bag at her feet.

She sees Hale across the lane. She lifts a hand.

She does not stand. He comes to her.

HALE
Mira.

MIRA
Inspector.

HALE
Joss.

MIRA
I know. I found him.

HALE
I know you did.

A beat.

MIRA
I have something for you.

She nudges the bag with her boot.

MIRA
A book. Stamped sigil. Cover's leather, gold tooling, black wax. Found it in the basement. High shelf. Tin bread-box.

HALE
You took it.

MIRA
I did.

HALE
Why didn't you give it to me yesterday.

MIRA
I had Joss to see to.

A long beat.

HALE
Mira. You should not have gone in.

MIRA
He was my brother.

HALE
Yes.

She lifts the bag and hands it to him. He looks at the sigil on the cover. A ringed compass-rose, gold and black. He has seen the same shape before, on a Council ring, walking past him in the evidence room corridor.

HALE
You know whose this is.

MIRA
I have a guess. You have the registry.

HALE
I have the registry.

He pulls a folded sheet from his coat — a cheap printer's copy of the Council registry of sigil-rings. He spreads it on the step beside her and points at row, by row, asking her to compare.

She runs her finger down the page. She stops at row four — the Eastern Ward. She taps the entry.

MIRA
That one. That's it.

HALE
You're sure.

MIRA
The ring's exact. I've seen it on a man's hand at the Salt Exchange steps a hundred times. Thane.

HALE
(quiet)
Thane.

MIRA
There's more I haven't told you. I'm not telling it today.

A beat.

HALE
When you're ready.

MIRA
When I'm ready.

He does not push. He goes.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 7 — INT. HARBOR PRECINCT — HALE'S OFFICE — EVENING

Hale at his desk. The Black Ledger spread open across the blotter. The pages are dense with column-work — names, dates, amounts, in a careful clerical hand.

He has the Council registry of sigil-rings open beside it.

The compass-rose on the cover matches. Council Seat 4 — the Eastern Ward — Thelonius Aurel Thane.

He turns to a random page. He reads names. *Brenn, A. Five hundred crowns, Salt-Tax Trust, March 1894. Brenn, A. Six hundred, May 1894. Brenn, A. — quarterly, indexed.*

A small, cold thing in his chest. He has known Aldous Brenn fourteen years.

He turns to the front of the Ledger. The first entry is dated 1881. Two years after the Surge. The first line reads, *Watch — silence — bulk salt — 14 D 1881 onward.*

He shuts the Ledger. He locks it in his desk drawer with the verses.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 8 — EXT. SOUTHERN POINT — THIRD HOUR — NIGHT

Verity, hooded, walks the wet sand. She has the notebook. She has the pencil. She has not eaten today.

She does not see, on the slope above the cove, a man crouched low in the deep shadow of a basalt outcrop, his oilcloth coat pulled around him, an almanac of the Lunar Tables in his pocket and a service revolver under his coat.

She kneels at the waterline. She lays her right palm flat. She lifts her left wrist.

She speaks. Her voice, this time, is barely a voice. The Tide-tongue is shaped more by breath than sound.

VERITY (Tide-tongue, subtitled)
Joss Vance. Drowned not, but bled. Carved, but not by me. Speak.

A long beat. The water listens.

A VOICE comes up. The same warm, careful woman in her early thirties.

VOICE FROM THE BAY (Eline Hale's voice, V.O.)
*The hand that held the iron key / was not the hand that turned. / The man who paid the dredgemen's salt / was not the one who burned.*

Four lines. Verity writes.

VOICE FROM THE BAY (cont'd)
*And the wife who walked the southern point / was not the wife who drowned.*

A fifth line.

The voice is quiet a moment. Then it speaks again, and what it says next is not a verse. It is a sentence.

VOICE FROM THE BAY (cont'd)
*Tell my husband.*

Verity's hand stops on the page.

She turns her head. Above her, on the slope, Hale has stood up.

He has heard the last line. He has, in fact, heard the entire stanza, because the Bay carries voices farther in moonlight than people in Vassen are willing to admit.

He walks down to the waterline.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 9 — EXT. SOUTHERN POINT — WATERLINE — CONTINUOUS

Verity does not stand. She is on her knees. The page is in her lap.

HALE
Verity.

She does not look at him.

HALE
(very quiet)
Tell me.

She lifts the notebook. She tears the page out. She holds it up to him without lifting her eyes.

He takes it. He reads. He recognizes the stanza by the third line. *The man who paid the dredgemen's salt / was not the one who burned.* It is, line for line, the fourth stanza of Eline's poem. He has it copied at the cottage.

He looks at Verity. He looks at the Bay.

HALE
You're a Tide Witch.

She nods, once. Her chin against her chest.

HALE
How long.

She lifts her left hand. Five fingers. She extends them again. Five more. She gestures *more.*

HALE
Since you were a girl.

She nods.

HALE
You spoke just now to the shade of Joss Vance.

She nods.

HALE
And the shade answered in another voice.

She nods.

HALE
A woman's voice.

She nods.

HALE
My wife's voice.

She closes her eyes. She nods.

A long beat. Hale crouches in the wet sand opposite her. He does not touch her.

HALE
The verses — every one of them — you've been drawing.

She nods.

HALE
For someone who instructed you.

She nods.

HALE
For someone who threatened your mother.

She nods.

HALE
The hooded one.

She nods.

HALE
Verity. Why a shade speaks in another's voice.

She does not nod. She shakes her head, slowly. She turns her gloved palm up. *I do not know.* She has been asking the same question for two nights and finding no answer in any rule of mnemoncraft or Tide-witchery she was taught.

She tries to speak.

VERITY
(barely audible)
I do not—

The word *know* does not come. Her throat closes. She tries it again. Nothing.

She lifts her gloved hand to her own throat and presses, hard. She tries once more.

Nothing.

She is silent. She will be silent for the rest of her life.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 10 — EXT. SOUTHERN POINT — WATERLINE — CONTINUOUS

She turns her notebook around. She writes, fast, in pencil—

NOTEBOOK INSERT (Verity's hand)
*My voice has gone. I should have had two more decades. I am being burned through. I do not know why.*

He reads. He nods, once.

He takes the notebook from her. He turns to a fresh page. He writes, in his own hand—

HALE'S WRITING (insert)
*Eline did not kill herself. I have suspected for five years. I now know.*

She reads. She closes her eyes.

He writes, below it—

*Who has been giving you the orders.*

She takes the notebook. She writes—

*A hooded courier. Once spoken to in a low voice. Once not. The hand wears no glove on the right. The right hand has a Council ring on the index finger. Compass-rose. Gold and black.*

He reads. He folds the page out of the notebook and slides it inside his coat lining with the warrant.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 11 — EXT. SOUTHERN POINT — LATER — NIGHT

They have walked, slowly, back toward Lighthouse Cottage. The tide is in. Verity has the notebook open in her gloved hands. She writes as they walk, by moonlight.

She tears out a page and hands it to him.

NOTEBOOK PAGE (insert)
*Five forged letters from my mother's physician. I have kept them. They are in my coat lining. I will give them to you in the morning. If anything happens to me tonight, the lining is sewn shut at the top.*

He reads. He puts the page in his pocket.

Above the cottage, the lantern over the door has gone out. He does not relight it.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 12 — INT. LIGHTHOUSE COTTAGE — KITCHEN — NIGHT

The deal table. Two cups of salt-water tea. Verity's notebook open between them. He talks; she writes.

HALE
The Compass is in the Council vault.

She writes: *Iron-roofed.*

HALE
Yes.

She writes: *Inside it doesn't work.*

HALE
That is the point of putting it there.

She writes: *Then we take it out.*

HALE
We take it out.

She writes: *He has my mother.*

A long beat.

HALE
Verity.

She lifts her eyes.

HALE
I do not believe he has her any longer.

She closes her eyes. She nods, once.

She writes, slowly: *Tomorrow night.*

He nods.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 13 — INT. DREDGEMASTER'S BARGE — A SMALL SAFE-ROOM — LATE NIGHT

Olen Brask, the Dredgemaster, in a low bunk. A bandage on his left forearm, fresh — he has fought off a knife in the harbor an hour ago. He is bleeding lightly. His nephew TOBIN BRASK, 22, lean, dark-haired, sits on a stool with a fish-knife of his own across his knees.

DREDGEMASTER
Tobin.

TOBIN
Uncle.

DREDGEMASTER
Pen.

Tobin hands him a stub of pencil and a strip of brown paper from the chart-table.

The Dredgemaster writes seven names in a careful, square hand. The third name is *Thelonius Thane.*

DREDGEMASTER
To Hale. By your hand. Tonight.

TOBIN
Tonight.

DREDGEMASTER
I'll be on a moonweed-trawler in the outer roads by sunrise. Don't follow. Don't be seen.

TOBIN
And you.

DREDGEMASTER
I'll be back when the Compass is broken. Not before.

Tobin nods. He takes the paper. He goes.

The Dredgemaster lies back. He puts his maimed left hand over his eyes.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 14 — EXT. LOWER WARDS — AN ALLEY — SAME LATE NIGHT

Mira, alone in the alley. She has been at the bay-burial preparations all evening; she is on her way home.

A figure ahead of her. Then another behind. Then a third on the rooftop. Black Watch. Three of them.

She reaches for the fish-knife in her boot.

The first Watch grabs her by the bandaged shoulder. She cries out — the only sound she will make in the fight. She drives the knife into his thigh. He drops her.

The second Watch is on her with a sap. She blocks once with her forearm; the second blow takes her at the temple. She goes down, conscious but blurred.

A gloved hand reaches into her jerkin. The Compass — which she has carried, against her own better judgment, since the Salt Exchange steps two nights ago — is taken.

A whistle from a constabulary patrol two streets away. Boots on stone.

The Watch run. They take the Compass. They leave Mira on the cobbles.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 15 — EXT. LOWER WARDS — ALLEY — MOMENTS LATER

A patrol constable, MARLEN, 30s, finds her. He kneels.

MARLEN
Vance.

MIRA
(through teeth)
Tell Hale they took it.

MARLEN
Took what.

MIRA
Tell him.

She passes out.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 16 — INT. HARBOR PRECINCT — INFIRMARY — VERY LATE

Mira on a cot. Verity at the bedside, gloved hand on Mira's wrist, taking the pulse the old way — by counting against her own breath, since she can no longer say a count aloud.

Hale stands at the window. He has Tobin Brask's folded paper in his hand. The seven names. *Thane.*

VERITY (writing on a slip, handing it to him)
*Compass back in the vault tomorrow. The Watch will deliver.*

HALE
We'll be there before they lock it.

VERITY (writing)
*How.*

HALE
Mira's roof-route. When she wakes.

A beat.

VERITY (writing)
*Tomorrow night. Last lunar working hour.*

HALE
Last.

She nods.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 17 — INT. THANE'S STUDY — VERY LATE — INTERCUT

A high-ceilinged room, lined in dark oak. A desk of black walnut. A single lamp.

THELONIUS THANE, 61, white-haired, narrow, in shirtsleeves at his desk. He has a leather portfolio open. From it, he removes a sheaf of folded pages — Eline Hale's verses, in Eline's hand.

He reads, slowly, the fourth stanza. He has read it many times. He reads it again, in case it has changed. It has not.

He folds it back. He returns it to the portfolio. He locks the portfolio in the desk drawer. The drawer also contains, in a velvet pouch, a plain gold wedding band.

He lifts the pouch. He holds the ring up to the lamplight. He turns it once.

He sets it back. He locks the drawer.

He does not yet know that the Black Ledger is missing from his cellar in the Eastern District. He will know in the morning, when his agent reports.

He pours himself a small glass of bergamot-scented brandy and drinks it slowly.

CUT TO:

# SCENE 18 — EXT. LIGHTHOUSE POINT — JUST BEFORE DAWN

Hale at the rail. The almanac in his coat. The garnet ring at his throat. The Black Ledger under his arm. Tobin's seven names in his pocket. Verity's note about the lining sewn shut in another pocket.

He looks at the Bay. He thinks of his wife's voice in the mouth of a boy he has watched grow up. He thinks of the hand that held the iron key.

He says her name aloud once. To the Bay. Not in a whisper.

HALE
Eline.

The Bay does not answer him. He is not a Tide Witch. He never will be.

Above him, the moon is past full and declining. The almanac says one more night of working hours.

Below him, on the lower stones of the seawall, the high-water mark sits an inch above where it sat the morning Marsh was found. The city is sinking, as it has been since 1879. He has noticed. He has not spoken of it.

The lantern on the cottage door is still out. He does not relight it.

FADE OUT.

END OF EPISODE 4.
