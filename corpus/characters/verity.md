# Sergeant Verity Crane

## Full Name
Verity Eilis Crane

## Role
Sergeant of Special Cases, Constabulary of Vassen. Hale's partner of seven years. Secretly the last living Tide Witch in Aelmar. Age 31. POV character for episode 3.

## Physical
Mid-height, lean, copper-red hair cropped short — unfashionable for a woman of her class, deliberately so. Sea-green eyes. A faint silvering at the right temple that a careful observer might mistake for early grey but is, in fact, the first sign of Tide-mark. Small, square hands; calloused along the right index finger from writing. Dresses in regulation constable greys; carries no jewelry except a thin silver bracelet at her left wrist, never removed, which is in fact a charm against the Tide-voices waking in daylight.

## Voice
Dry, fast, sometimes sharper than she means. Asks questions. Interrupts witnesses. Laughs once per episode at most, never warmly. By episode 4 she stops speaking aloud entirely; from episode 5 onward she communicates only by writing. The first sign of her voice failing is a hoarseness in episode 3 that Hale assumes is a cold.

## Knowledge State Across Episodes

**Pre-series:**
- Has known she is a Tide Witch since age fourteen, when her dying grandmother told her on a moonlit beach.
- Has practiced in secret all her adult life, always alone, always at the southern point near Lighthouse Cottage when Hale is on the night shift.
- Knows the Lantern Edict makes what she is illegal; if discovered, she would hang.
- Knows Eline Hale by reputation; met her twice, briefly, before her death.
- Does NOT know Eline ever practiced mnemoncraft.
- Does NOT know her own mother is dying. Believes her mother is in stable convalescence at a Council-funded sanitarium, on Councilor Thane's personal patronage.

**Episode 1:**
- Learns: Edric Marsh is dead in the Eastern District with a verse carved into him.
- Reads the verse over Hale's shoulder at the scene; recognizes nothing — does not know the verse is Eline's.
- Receives, by courier, a sealed letter from her mother's physician saying her mother has taken a turn and Thane has generously increased his patronage. The letter is forged.
- Does NOT know she is being set up.

**Episode 2:**
- Learns the second body's particulars; the cistern victim was a Black Watch corporal she had once arrested for assault.
- Is approached, in the small hours, by a hooded figure who tells her, in writing, that her mother will die in agony unless Verity does precisely as instructed: walk to the Bay at moonrise, speak to the drowned shade the figure names, and write down what the shade says.
- Walks to the Bay. Speaks to Edric Marsh's shade. The shade speaks in Eline Hale's voice and gives her a stanza of verse. Verity writes it down. The figure takes it.
- This is the first time Verity has heard a shade speak in another shade's voice. She does not understand why and is frightened.
- Does NOT know the figure is Thane's agent. Does NOT know her mother is already dead.

**Episode 3 (POV):**
- Performs the working a second time, against the corporal's shade. Receives the third stanza, in Eline's voice. Hands it to the figure.
- Notices her own voice going hoarse — earlier than the Tide-mark should have struck her, given her age. Does not yet understand why.
- Begins to suspect she is being lied to but cannot afford to test it: any deviation, the letter said, and her mother dies that night.
- Sees Mira Vance briefly at the Bay's edge but does not realize Mira saw her working.
- Does NOT yet know that Hale is becoming suspicious. Does NOT yet know the verses are Eline's.

**Episode 4:**
- Hale follows her to the Bay and sees her working. The cover is broken.
- Hale tells her the verses are Eline's, in Eline's hand. Verity, who has been transcribing the verses for the figure, realizes the implication: the shade she has been speaking to has been channeling Eline. She does not understand why a drowned shade can be made to speak in another's voice — this should not be possible under any rule of mnemoncraft or Tide-witchery she knows.
- Loses her speaking voice entirely, mid-conversation with Hale, on the beach. Communicates by writing thereafter.
- Does NOT yet know her mother is dead.

**Episode 5:**
- Discovers, in Thane's house, her mother's body — three weeks dead, kept on ice. The letters were forged.
- Confronts Thane. He admits everything: he killed Eline in 1898 because she had found, in the Salt Exchange's sealed records, proof that the Eastern District had been deliberately flooded by Council order to bury a crime; he kept her ghost in the Bay; he knew Verity was a Tide Witch and had used the patronage of her mother as long-term leverage.
- Helps Hale destroy the Vassen Compass in the iron-roofed Council vault, ending Thane's grip on the Council.
- Speaks, with effort, exactly one sentence at the end of episode 5: "She wanted you to know."

## Possessions

- **Silver bracelet, thin, left wrist.** Charm against daylight Tide-voices. Worn since age fourteen. Never removed.
- **Constabulary badge, brass, number 88.**
- **Service revolver.** Rarely carried; she prefers a short iron baton.
- **A pocket notebook, oilcloth-bound.** Used for case notes and, from episode 4 onward, for all her speech.
- **Her grandmother's signed gesture-book.** A small leather codex of Tide-witch hand-signs. Hidden under her floorboards. Retrieved in episode 5.
- **The forged letters from her "mother."** Five of them, kept hidden in her coat lining until episode 4, when she gives them to Hale.

## Relationships
- **Casimir Hale.** Partner. The closest person to her in the world; she has nonetheless kept the central fact of her life from him for seven years.
- **Eilan Crane** (mother, age 58). Bedridden five years before the season opens with what Verity believed was a wasting illness; in fact, Thane had been having her slow-poisoned through her medications, on and off, to keep her chronically ill but alive. He stopped feeding her the keeping-poison three weeks before episode 1, knowing she would die quickly.
- **Thelonius Thane.** Her family's "patron." Has known her since she was a girl. She has dined at his table four times in her life. She did not know what he was until episode 5.
- **Mira Vance.** Knows of her as Hale's informant. Did not know Mira saw her working until episode 5.

## Secrets
- She is a Tide Witch (revealed to Hale, ep04).
- She has been transcribing verses from a forced shade (revealed to Hale, ep04).
- She has, three times in her life, contacted her grandmother's shade in the Bay; her grandmother told her, the last time, "the man who pays your mother's doctor is the one who will end your voice." Verity did not understand at the time. She does now.
