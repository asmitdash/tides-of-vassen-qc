# The Dredgemaster

## Full Name
Olen Marrow Brask, styled "the Dredgemaster of Vassen." Age 63.

## Role
Hereditary keeper of the harbor channels and, by ancient grant, custodian of the Vassen Compass. Not a Councilor; reports to the Council quarterly. Operates from a barge-office moored at the head of the shipping channel.

## Physical
Squat, barrel-chested, weathered to leather. Bald with a salt-white beard cut square at the collarbone. Missing the third and fourth fingers of his left hand, lost to a dredge-chain in 1881. Walks with a heavy iron-tipped cane that doubles as an improvised cosh. Wears the Dredgemaster's brass chain of office over a plain canvas coat — refuses ceremonial dress; the chain is the only mark of his rank.

## Voice
Loud. Bargemen's accent — flattened vowels, dropped final consonants. Curses fluently in three languages. Speaks affectionately of the Bay as "she" and of the Council as "those bastards." Will not lie within seven paces of the Compass; refuses, on principle, to lie at all when sober.

## Knowledge State Across Episodes

**Pre-series:**
- Knows the harbor and the Bay's tides better than any living person.
- Holds the Vassen Compass in a salt-cedar box bolted to his desk; checks it weekly to confirm the needle still moves.
- Has known Casimir Hale's father (also a constable) since boyhood; tolerated Hale himself since Hale's promotion in 1893.
- Knows that the Eastern District flooding in 1879 was *not* a natural surge — he was a young dredgeman that night and saw the harbor sluices opened from the inside. Has never told anyone. Has been paid handsomely, every year since 1881, in salt and silence.
- Does NOT know who specifically gave the order to open the sluices. Suspects the Council collectively.

**Episode 1:**
- Hears of the Eastern District body within the hour; the bargemen's network is faster than the constabulary.
- Tells Hale, when Hale visits, that he saw "nothing, never, get off my barge." Lies — the first time the Compass needle has swung against him in a decade. He notices. Hale does not.
- The Compass needle, at the lie, swings to point at the Dredgemaster's own chest. He covers it with his hand.

**Episode 2:**
- The Compass is stolen from the salt-cedar box during the night. The box is forced open with an iron pry-bar; the bolts are sheared.
- The Dredgemaster wakes to find the box open, the Compass gone, and a single grey feather on the desk — the marker of the Black Watch.
- Reports the theft to Hale immediately. Tells Hale, for the first time, the truth about 1879. Names no specific Councilor; tells Hale that whoever ordered the sluices opened is, by his guess, still alive and still on the Council.
- This conversation is overheard by no one. The Dredgemaster does not write it down.

**Episode 3:**
- Hears, through the bargemen, that the Compass has surfaced briefly in the Lower Wards; a salt-runner girl has it. (This is Mira.) Sends word to Hale.
- Receives a visit from a Black Watch corporal demanding to know what he has told the Constabulary. The Dredgemaster, drunk and unwise, tells the corporal to the corporal's face that he knows the Watch opened the sluices in '79 and that the Council paid for it. The corporal leaves without violence.
- That night, the corporal's body is found in a freshwater cistern. The Dredgemaster realizes, too late, that he has just gotten a man killed.
- (Note: the corporal's death is *also* attributed to the verse-killer; the Dredgemaster's revelation accelerated, but did not cause, the killing.)

**Episode 4:**
- Survives an attempt on his life — a hired knife in the harbor, fought off with the iron cane.
- Goes into hiding aboard a moonweed-trawler in the outer roads.
- Sends Hale, by hand of his nephew, a sealed envelope containing a list of seven names: the seven Councilors he believes were alive and seated in 1879. Thane's name is on it.

**Episode 5:**
- Returns to Vassen the morning of the final confrontation. Helps Hale and Verity gain access to the Council Chamber's lower vault — he holds a Dredgemaster's key by ancient right.
- Witnesses the destruction of the Compass. Does not mourn it.
- Final line of the season is his, spoken to the Bay at low tide: "Keep her, then. She was never ours."

## Possessions

- **The Vassen Compass.** Held until episode 2. Stolen ep02, recovered briefly by Mira ep03, seized by Black Watch acting for Thane ep04, destroyed in the Council vault ep05.
- **Salt-cedar box, brass-bolted.** Compass's container. Splintered after the theft, never replaced.
- **Brass chain of office.** Worn always.
- **Iron-tipped cane.** Functional and ceremonial. Has cracked four men's skulls in his lifetime by his own count.
- **Dredgemaster's key, iron, three-toothed.** Opens the lower vault of the Council Chamber by ancient grant. Held on a cord under his shirt.
- **A leather logbook of harbor depths,** dating from 1873. The first three pages document the night of 14 Drimmar 1879; the relevant entries are written so faintly they require a strong lamp to read. He has never destroyed them.

## Relationships
- **Casimir Hale.** Tolerates. Trusted by ep05.
- **The Council of Nine.** Despised, paid by, bound to.
- **His nephew, Tobin Brask** (age 22). Apprentice and courier. Survives the season.
- **The Bay itself.** He speaks of it as a person. He is, in some quiet way, in love with it.

## Secrets
- He saw the harbor sluices opened from the inside on the night of the Tide Surge. He has accepted Council bribes to stay silent for twenty-two years. He does not feel guilt for the bribes — he feels guilt for having waited.
- The Compass needle swung at his own lie in episode 1 for the first time in a decade. He understood, in that moment, that the price of his silence had finally come due.
