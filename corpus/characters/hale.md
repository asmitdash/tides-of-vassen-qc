# Inspector Casimir Hale

## Full Name
Casimir Aldric Hale

## Role
Inspector of Special Cases, Constabulary of Vassen. Protagonist. POV character for episodes 1, 2, 4. Age 47.

## Physical
Tall, narrow-shouldered, grey-streaked black hair worn long enough to tie back. Pale grey eyes. A two-inch scar across the bridge of the nose, taken in 1894 from a fish-hook. Walks with a slight favor of the left knee in cold weather. Dresses in constable greys: oilcloth coat, iron-shod boots, leather gloves even indoors. Wears his late wife Eline's wedding ring on an iron chain at his throat from episode 2 onward, after his own gold band is stolen.

## Voice
Quiet. Speaks in short declaratives. Almost never asks a question he does not already half-know the answer to. Uses people's surnames, never given names, except for Verity. The single word he says most often across the season is "Tell me."

## Knowledge State Across Episodes

**Pre-series:**
- Knows Vassen, the Lantern Edict, mnemoncraft theory, and standard constabulary procedure.
- Knows his wife Eline Hale died on the night of the 3rd of Drimmar, 1898, drowned in Vassen Bay; the cause was officially ruled suicide.
- Does NOT know Eline ever practiced mnemoncraft.
- Does NOT know the verses she wrote in 1897 exist outside his own memory; he believes the only copy was burned with her papers.

**Episode 1:**
- Learns: a Council clerk, Edric Marsh, has been found in the Eastern District (entry illegal) with a five-line verse carved into his sternum and a cracked memory-stone in his mouth.
- Recognizes the verse as one of Eline's, in her handwriting, though carved.
- Loses: his own gold wedding ring is stolen from the evidence locker before he can examine the body's effects properly. (He had set it aside while gloving up.)
- Does NOT know the killer's identity; suspects no one specific yet.

**Episode 2:**
- Learns: a second body — a Black Watch corporal — found drowned in a freshwater cistern in the Lower Wards, with a second stanza of Eline's verse on the chest.
- Learns: the Vassen Compass, a Council artifact held by the Dredgemaster, has been stolen.
- Learns from the Drowned Mother's Order that there were exactly two copies of Eline's 1897 verses; one was Hale's; the other was held by a person whose name the Order will not give him without a Council warrant.
- Begins wearing Eline's ring on the iron chain.
- Does NOT yet know Verity is the Tide Witch.

**Episode 3:**
- Learns: a third body — Mira's older brother, Joss Vance — found in the Eastern District. The verse on him is the third stanza, and it is fresh (carved within the hour).
- Learns: the Compass was last seen in the hand of the Dredgemaster at the moment of theft; Mira witnessed the theft.
- Learns: the killer carves only on moonlit nights.
- Does NOT yet know Verity is the Tide Witch. Does NOT yet know Thane is involved.
- Suspects the second copy of Eline's verses was held by Councilor Thane; cannot prove it.

**Episode 4:**
- Learns, definitively, that **Verity Crane is the last living Tide Witch.** This is the central revelation of the season for him. He learns it when he sees her speak to the drowned shade of Edric Marsh in the moonlight at the Bay's edge, and the shade answers in Eline's voice.
- Learns: the killer has been forcing Verity, by leverage Hale does not yet understand, to draw Eline's verses out of her drowned shade and pass them along.
- Receives the Black Ledger from Mira, who took it from Lighthouse Cottage in episode 3. The Ledger documents twenty-two years of Thane's bribes to the Black Watch, the Salt Exchange, and three other Councilors, all to keep the Eastern District sealed.
- Now suspects, but cannot prove, that the leverage on Verity is her dying mother, held in Thane's house under medical pretense.

**Episode 5:**
- Confirms the leverage: Verity's mother has been dead for three weeks; Thane forged the letters Verity received. Verity learns this at the same moment Hale does.
- Learns: Eline went into the Eastern District the night she died, voluntarily, having discovered something Thane wanted buried. Her death was not suicide. Thane knew this. Hale's official report — which he wrote and signed — was wrong.
- Recovers his stolen gold wedding ring from Thane's desk drawer.
- Receives, from the dying Thane, a single uncracked memory-stone — Eline's last memory, taken from her shade by Verity under duress — and chooses not to read it. The Compass is destroyed in the Council Chamber's iron-roofed vault during the final confrontation.

## Possessions

- **Gold wedding band, plain.** Worn since 1882. Stolen from evidence locker, episode 1. Recovered from Thane's desk, episode 5. Never worn again — kept in a tin box on his mantel.
- **Eline's wedding ring (silver, garnet).** Worn on iron chain at throat from episode 2 onward.
- **Constabulary badge, brass, number 41.** Always on his person.
- **Service revolver, six-shot.** Carried under coat. Fired three times in the season — once in ep03, twice in ep05.
- **A folded copy of Eline's 1897 verses, in his own handwriting.** Kept in a locked drawer at Lighthouse Cottage. Untouched until episode 3, when he checks the page numbers.
- **An almanac of the Lunar Tables, 1903 edition.** Carries it in his coat pocket from episode 2 onward; checks it before every night-walk.
- **An iron flask, plain.** Holds salt-water tea.

## Relationships
- **Eline Hale** (wife, deceased 1898). The absence around which his life is built. His only living connection to her is the memory of her voice and the verses he can recite from memory.
- **Verity Crane** (partner, age 31). Three years his junior in service, four in age. They have worked together for seven years. He calls her by her given name. He does not yet know what she is.
- **Mira Vance** (informant, age 19, Lower Wards). A salt-runner who passes him news from the docks. Becomes a witness in episode 3.
- **Councilor Thelonius Thane.** Hale has met him three times in his career, all at official functions. Hale dislikes him without being able to articulate why; by season's end, the reason is clear.
- **Commissioner Aldous Brenn.** Hale's superior. A decent man who is also, quietly, in Thane's pocket. Does not know he is.

## Secrets
- He has never told anyone, including Verity, that Eline's body was missing its left hand when it was recovered. The official report omits this.
- He has read his wife's verses aloud, to himself, once a year on the anniversary of her death, every year since 1898.
- He believes — correctly, though without proof until ep05 — that he was the one who insisted Eline take the walk that ended in her drowning, because he had refused to listen to her that afternoon.
