# Councilor Thelonius Thane

## Full Name
Thelonius Aurel Thane, Councilor of the Eastern Ward, Council of Nine.

## Role
Antagonist. Hereditary Councilor since 1887, age 61. Architect of the cover-up of the Tide Surge of 1879, murderer of Eline Hale in 1898, principal manipulator of the events of the season.

## Physical
Tall, narrow, immaculate. White hair worn longer than fashion permits, brushed back from a high forehead. Pale, almost colorless eyes — described in episode 1 as "the color of brackish water at dawn." Long-fingered hands; manicured. Wears Council black with a high-collared white shirt, an ebony cane he does not need, and his sigil ring (a ringed compass-rose, gold and black enamel) on the right index finger. Smells faintly of bergamot and dry paper.

## Voice
Cultivated. Slow. Selects words like a man choosing rings from a tray. Never raises his voice; the only time it cracks is in episode 5, and only once. Capable of reciting from memory any of the eighty-seven articles of the Lantern Edict.

## Knowledge State Across Episodes

**Pre-series:**
- Knows that on the night of 14 Drimmar 1879, the harbor sluices were opened from inside on the order of his father (then Councilor for the Eastern Ward) and three other Councilors, in order to drown the Eastern District. The reason: an audit, due the following week, would have uncovered two decades of embezzlement of Crown salt-tax revenues by the same four Councilors, with the records held in the Eastern District guildhalls. The flood destroyed the records and killed every clerk who could have testified.
- Knows that his father's role in the order is documented in his own personal ledger — the Black Ledger — which he keeps because, in his own private logic, a man should know what he has done.
- Knows that in 1898 Eline Hale, working as a copyist at the Salt Exchange under contract, found in the sealed accounts a discrepancy that pointed at the Eastern Ward records and at his father's name. She wrote to him asking for an explanation. He invited her to walk with him on the southern point on the night of the 3rd of Drimmar, 1898. He drowned her in the shallows near Lighthouse Cottage and made it appear a suicide. He took her left hand, which bore an inkstain on the index finger from her copyist's work and would have proven she had been writing in the sealed records that day. He weighted the body and pushed it into the channel.
- Knows Verity Crane is a Tide Witch — has known since she was twelve, when his then-physician, who attended her grandmother in her final illness, sold him the information.
- Has paid for Eilan Crane (Verity's mother)'s medical care for nineteen years as long-term leverage, slowly poisoning her on and off through her medications to keep her chronically ill but alive.
- Holds the only other copy of Eline Hale's 1897 verses — taken from her body the night he drowned her. He has read them many times. They are, against his expectation, beautiful.

**Episode 1:**
- Has just had Edric Marsh killed. Marsh was a Council clerk who, three weeks earlier, had begun unofficially auditing Eastern Ward shipping manifests on his own initiative and was about to find what Eline Hale found.
- Carved the verse on Marsh himself — he is the killer's hand, not just the orchestrator. The handwriting on the carved verse is in fact Marsh's own writing rendered into verse-form by Verity's working two nights later — but Thane planted the body, the cracked memory-stone, and the staging.
- (Note: the verses are *carved* in stanzas matching Eline's poem. Thane carves them; Verity, forced afterward, draws fresh stanzas from the dead's shades to give Thane the next set. The order is: Thane kills, then Verity is forced to extract the next stanza from the new shade, then Thane uses it on the next victim. The verses move forward through the poem episode by episode.)
- Stops feeding Eilan Crane the keeping-poison; she will die in roughly three weeks.
- Sets up the forged letters to Verity.

**Episode 2:**
- Has the Black Watch steal the Vassen Compass from the Dredgemaster. He wants the Compass off the board because, if turned on him in any room, it would point at his chest. The theft is staged with sufficient evidence (the grey feather) to make the source unmistakable to the Dredgemaster but uncertain to Hale.
- Sends his agent to Verity. Verity walks to the Bay and produces the second stanza.
- Has the Black Watch corporal killed because the corporal is asking too many questions of the Dredgemaster. Carves the verse himself.

**Episode 3:**
- Eilan Crane dies, three weeks earlier than Verity is being told. Thane has the body kept on ice in his own house — a calculation: he may need to produce it at short notice and does not want decomposition to complicate the timing.
- Has Joss Vance killed. Joss had been moving smuggled cargo through the Eastern District and had stumbled into a Black Watch checkpoint near Thane's old cellar — the cellar where the Black Ledger was kept. Thane has Joss killed both to silence him and to choose the Eastern District basement as the body's location, putting the third stanza there for symbolic weight.
- Does NOT know that Mira finds the Black Ledger that night. Believes the cellar still holds it.
- Discovers the Compass briefly missing again; the Watch courier dropped it. Has it recovered ep04.

**Episode 4:**
- Confirms, through his agents, that Hale has discovered Verity's nature. Decides this is acceptable; Verity is now a tool too compromised to expose him without exposing herself.
- Recovers the Compass from Mira's alley. Locks it in the Council Chamber's iron-roofed vault, where it cannot work.
- Realizes the Black Ledger is missing from the cellar; cannot determine when or by whom. Begins, for the first time, to plan a graceful retreat.

**Episode 5:**
- Confronted in his house by Hale and Verity. Verity finds her mother's body in the cold-room. Confronted with this, Thane's voice cracks for the only time in the season. He confesses everything — to Verity directly, in Hale's hearing — because he has decided that the audience for his confession should be the woman whose voice he has spent twenty years preparing to take.
- Dies in the Council vault, shot once by Hale after attempting to seize the Compass and use it as a hostage. Hands Hale, in his last minute, an uncracked memory-stone — Eline's last memory, taken by Verity's working under duress, the moment of her drowning.
- His final words: "Read it. You should know."

## Possessions

- **Sigil ring, gold and black enamel, ringed compass-rose.** On right index finger always. Stamps every official Council vote.
- **The Black Ledger.** Twenty-two years of bribes, names, dates, amounts. Hidden in a cellar in the abandoned Eastern District until ep03, taken by Mira ep03, given to Hale ep04, used as evidence ep05.
- **Eline Hale's left hand,** preserved in a glass jar of brine and silver salt in a locked cabinet in his study. Discovered by Hale and Verity ep05.
- **The other copy of Eline's 1897 verses,** kept in a leather portfolio on his desk. Taken by Hale ep05.
- **Hale's stolen gold wedding ring,** kept in the top drawer of his desk in a velvet pouch. (He took it himself, personally, from the evidence locker in ep01, by Council privilege of inspection. He kept it as a souvenir.) Recovered by Hale ep05.
- **Ebony cane, silver-shod.** Affectation. Never used as weapon.
- **A signed copy of the 1881 Lantern Edict, original parchment.** Hangs framed in his study.

## Relationships
- **Eline Hale** (deceased 1898). His victim. The only person in his life he has, in private, admitted to admiring.
- **Casimir Hale.** A man Thane has watched closely for five years and has dismissed, repeatedly, as a grief-blunted instrument. Underestimates him until ep05.
- **Verity Crane.** A long-term project. He thinks of her, in his private logic, as the voice he has been preparing.
- **Eilan Crane.** A woman whose name he has spoken, by his own count, fewer than ten times in his life, all of them in writing.
- **The other three Councilors who ordered the sluices opened in 1879.** All dead by 1903 — two of natural causes, one of a dueling accident Thane arranged.
- **Commissioner Brenn.** In his pocket since 1894. Brenn does not know he is in anyone's pocket — Thane's bribes are routed through a fake charitable trust.

## Secrets
- He killed Eline Hale.
- He keeps her hand.
- He has the verses.
- He has been poisoning Eilan Crane for nineteen years.
- His father, not himself, gave the order in 1879 — but he has continued the cover-up by his own choice for two decades and considers the crime, by inheritance and ratification, his own.
