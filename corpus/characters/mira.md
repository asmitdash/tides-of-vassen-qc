# Mira Vance

## Full Name
Mira Pell Vance

## Role
Salt-runner, informant, witness. Lower Wards native. Age 19. POV character for episode 5.

## Physical
Small, wiry, brown-skinned, sun-darkened from rooftop work. Black hair in a single thick braid down her back. A jagged scar across the right palm, taken from climbing a rusted gutter at twelve. Dresses for movement: canvas trousers, oilcloth jerkin, soft-soled boots stitched by her brother. Carries a fish-knife in her left boot. Has never owned a coat that fit her.

## Voice
Quick, low, accented Lower Wards — drops articles, runs words together, pitches up at the end of a sentence whether or not it is a question. Slightly out of breath even when she is not. Does not curse; her brother Joss did the cursing for both of them.

## Knowledge State Across Episodes

**Pre-series:**
- Knows the rooftops and gutters of the Lower Wards better than any constable.
- Has been one of Hale's informants for two years, since he caught her stealing salt from a Council storeroom and let her go in exchange for a promise of news.
- Knows her brother Joss runs with a smuggling crew that occasionally moves contraband out of the sealed Eastern District. Disapproves but does not interfere.
- Does NOT know about the Lantern Edict's enforcement priorities. Does NOT know what the Vassen Compass is, by name, until episode 2.

**Episode 1:**
- Hears the body has been found in the Eastern District; passes the rumor to Hale through their drop-spot at the Salt Exchange wall.
- Does NOT see the body. Does NOT know about the verse.

**Episode 2:**
- Climbs onto the Dredgemaster's barge roof on the night of the theft, intending to filch a coil of dredge-rope. Witnesses a single Black Watch corporal force the salt-cedar box and take a small brass instrument from inside. Does not know the instrument is the Compass at this point.
- Follows the corporal across two streets before losing him at the Council Wall.
- Tells Hale the next morning: a man in oilcloth-black took something brass from the Dredgemaster.
- That same night her brother Joss does not come home.

**Episode 3:**
- Goes looking for Joss in the Eastern District, breaching the wall through a gap she has known about since she was twelve.
- Finds Joss's body in a flooded basement. The third stanza of Eline's verse is freshly carved on his chest. He has been dead under an hour. The killer is gone.
- In the same basement, in a tin bread-box on a high shelf, finds a heavy leather book stamped with a sigil she does not recognize: the Black Ledger. (The basement is, unbeknownst to her, the cellar of a building Thane owned before the Tide Surge — he kept the Ledger there because the building is officially abandoned.)
- Takes the Ledger. Hides it under her own floorboards.
- Sees Verity Crane at the Bay's edge that same night, working — speaking to a shade in the moonlight. Does not understand what she is seeing. Tells no one.
- Recovers, separately, the Vassen Compass: she sees a Black Watch courier drop it accidentally on the Salt Exchange steps. She picks it up before he can. Carries it the rest of the night.

**Episode 4:**
- Gives Hale the Black Ledger. Tells him where she found it. Does not tell him about Verity at the Bay; she has decided that is not her secret to tell.
- The Compass is taken from her — Black Watch corner her in an alley; she fights, loses, the Compass is taken back. She survives because a passing constabulary patrol scares the Watch off.
- Identifies, from a Council registry of sigil-rings Hale shows her, the sigil on the Black Ledger: it is Councilor Thane's.

**Episode 5 (POV):**
- Helps Hale and Verity infiltrate the Council Chamber by the rooftop route — a path she has used since age fourteen.
- Witnesses Thane's confession and death. Witnesses the destruction of the Compass.
- Buries her brother in the bay-burial at the season's end. Receives, from Hale, an unexpected gift: a constabulary cadet's letter of consideration, signed by Hale's hand. She has not yet decided whether she will accept it.

## Possessions

- **Fish-knife, plain steel, in the left boot.** Carried since age fourteen.
- **A small clay charm of the Drowned Mother,** worn on a leather cord at her throat. A gift from her mother before her mother died in 1898 — the same season as Eline Hale, of the same wasting fever that took thirty-eight Lower Wards adults that winter.
- **The Black Ledger.** In her possession from ep03 (Joss's basement) through ep04 (handed to Hale). Never read by her — she cannot read fluently enough to follow Council shorthand.
- **The Vassen Compass.** In her possession briefly in ep03 (Salt Exchange steps to dawn) and lost ep04.
- **A folded sketch of her brother Joss,** age sixteen, drawn by a Lower Wards street-artist for a copper crown. Kept in her jerkin pocket. The only image she has of him.
- **A coil of climber's line, hemp,** wrapped around her waist under the jerkin.

## Relationships
- **Casimir Hale.** Her handler, her quiet friend. The only adult in the Upper City who has ever, in her hearing, called her by her given name without a sneer.
- **Joss Vance** (brother, age 24). Killed ep03. Her last family.
- **Verity Crane.** Knows of her as Hale's partner. After ep03, knows what she is. Has chosen to keep that knowledge.
- **The Dredgemaster.** Has known him since she was a child running the harbor; he has, three times in her life, fed her when no one else would. She does not steal from his barge. (The dredge-rope she went for in ep02 was, by her code, a loan.)

## Secrets
- She knows what Verity is, from ep03 onward, and has told no one.
- She picked Hale's pocket once, in their first year of acquaintance, and returned the wallet anonymously the next day. Hale knows. Has never mentioned it.
- She has, hidden behind a brick in her room, a single uncracked memory-stone she found on her brother's body, charged but unread. She does not know what is in it. She will not give it up. Hale does not know it exists.
